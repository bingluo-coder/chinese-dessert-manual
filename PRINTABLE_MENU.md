## Chagee Tea: 2 Tea Packages

The tea sets are for sipping slowly, cold brewing, or making playful drinks with milk, fruit, or sparkling water.

### Package 1: Six-Flavor Classic Tea Collection

<img src="chagee/package1/Screenshot 2026-06-24 211133.png" alt="Chagee six-flavor classic tea collection" width="280">

Includes Ceylon black tea, Longjing green tea, jasmine green tea, tea-blossom oolong, honeydew oolong, and peach oolong.

**Hot brew:** 220 ml water at 90 C, steep 3-5 minutes.

**Cold brew:** 400 ml cold water, add ice or refrigerate, steep about 20 minutes.

### Package 2: Cold-Brew Flavored Tea Set

<img src="chagee/pacakge2/Screenshot 2026-06-24 211655.png" alt="Chagee cold-brew flavored tea set" width="280">

Includes grape jasmine green tea, peach oolong tea, and floral black tea.

**Shake brew:** add 500 ml water, shake 30 seconds, then steep 3-10 minutes.

**Fun ideas:** try sparkling tea, milk tea, or fruit tea with ice.

## Local Chongqing Snacks

These are typical local snacks we eat often in Chongqing: casual, crunchy, spicy, sweet, salty, and full of Sichuan pepper personality.

### Guaiwei Hudou: Spicy-Sweet Broad Beans

<img src="chongqingsnaks/guaiweihudou.png" alt="Guaiwei Hudou spicy-sweet broad beans" width="260">

Crunchy broad beans with a "guaiwei" mixed flavor: sweet, salty, spicy, savory, and lightly numbing.

### Chen Changyin Mahua

<img src="chongqingsnaks/mahua.jpeg" alt="Chen Changyin assorted mahua" width="260">

Twisted fried dough snacks in assorted flavors such as original, honey, brown sugar, pepper-salt, and spicy guaiwei.

### Angel Potato Chips: Sichuan Pepper Spicy Flavor

<img src="chongqingsnaks/tianshitudoupian.avif" alt="Angel potato chips Sichuan pepper spicy flavor" width="260">

Crispy potato chips with chili and Sichuan pepper for a spicy, fragrant, lightly numbing taste.
